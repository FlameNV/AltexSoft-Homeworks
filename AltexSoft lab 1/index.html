There’s nothing hackers love more than a dismissed update reminder. We keep our team’s computers in shape and up to date so that they aren’t subject to attacks that could otherwise be avoided. Not properly updating your systems makes you an easy to reach target and hackers will often go after the lowest hanging fruit. When it comes to maintaining a strong cybersecurity network the best thing a user can do is to prevent problems before they occur, and the easiest way to do that is by making sure that all systems are updated and updated regularly.

51% of breaches involved malware, we cover your bases. Malware is classified as software that is designed with the intention of causing damage to your devices, whether those be phones, servers, laptops, or any other kind of device in the IoT. Malware often takes the form of code, innocuous content, scripts or software. With SKOUT’s End-to-End Protection you can rest assured that all vulnerable points within your system are accounted for and safeguarded so that you don’t fall victim to malware of any kind.

Based on the 2018 Verizon Data Breach Report

With 43% of breaches involving social attacks, cyber security is a team sport. We get your employees up to speed. Our maturity model makes sure that every team is able to set security benchmarks and reach those benchmarks as certain cybersecurity requirements are met. Requirements could range from individuals setting up approved firewalls to everyone within a team taking on a complex password system. Small changes often make all the difference when trying to avoid a cybersecurity crisis and will allow you to find trouble before trouble finds you.

i

Based on the 2018 Verizon Data Breach Report

81% of attacks involve stolen or weak credentials. The easiest way to protect yourself from having your credentials stolen is to make sure that they are not easy to replicate. This means including multiple characters and figures in your passwords, and not reusing the same passwords for multiple accounts. Also, it’s key to never store your passwords in one central location. Committing passwords to memory is often the best way to make sure that intruders don’t figure them out. All of these tips represent easy steps that you can take towards making your credentials stronger.
